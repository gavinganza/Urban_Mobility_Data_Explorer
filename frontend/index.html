Key Insights

Three data-driven findings from NYC taxi patterns

Speed & Congestion
NYC slows down during rush hours — but not when you'd expect
Average taxi speed drops significantly during evening rush (4–8 PM), yet morning commute speeds often rival midday traffic. The radar chart below reveals how the city breathes through its 24-hour cycle.
~12 mph typical Manhattan evening rush speed
Tipping Behaviour
Credit card passengers tip ~18% — cash riders show $0 reported
A striking divide exists between payment methods: credit card trips record consistent tip percentages, while cash trips show near-zero tip amounts in the dataset — likely due to cash tips being unreported rather than absent.
~18% average credit card tip percentage
Zone Concentration
Top 10 zones generate the majority of all NYC taxi revenue
Trip demand is heavily concentrated in Midtown Manhattan zones (Times Square, Penn Station, Midtown East) and the two airports. This hyper-concentration shapes driver strategy, surge pricing, and city infrastructure decisions.
Midtown + Airports dominant demand generators
